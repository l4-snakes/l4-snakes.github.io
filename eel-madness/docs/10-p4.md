# P4: grades, combos, LOVE, and a seafloor everywhere

The P4 build (spec agreed 2026-07-05, docs/07 "P4 ideation" is the idea log;
this doc is the implementation spec). Ships: food grades, food combos + the
stamina bar, the red-beans-and-rice patch, the LOVE axis, seafloor terrain on
every plane with a real depth-of-field blur (the one framebuffer exception),
shatterable rocks + the dressing shaker, and two new kelp types.

Deliberately NOT in this build: the combo's real reward (placeholder: charge
boost stamina — the reward call sits behind one function in main.js so it can
change), new picture-based foods (blocked on art), plant-hidden shaker
placements, tunnels, seafloor creatures/anemones.

## Food grades (common / rare / legendary)

Every spawned item (and every patch) rolls a **grade** — independent of the
`rarity` spawn-weight column. `tuning.GRADES`:

- `P`: roll probabilities (`rare` ~10%, `legendary` ~2%; common is the rest).
- `MUL`: the grade's progression-amount multiplier (1 / 2.5 / 6). Applied at
  eat time in main.js, so the CSV amounts stay authored as-is; the eat pulse
  and flash/shake ride the multiplied amount — a legendary bite *reads* big.
- **Tells while falling** (render-only offsets — physics, probe, and eat
  checks all use the true position):
  - rare+ — **buzz**: a per-item random jitter target resampled continuously,
    exponentially smoothed (`BUZZ_A` px, `BUZZ_TAU` s).
  - legendary — buzz + **throb**: a size pulse that is NOT a sine —
    `THROB_DUTY` of the period is a smooth swell (sin² bump), the rest is
    dwell at rest size (`THROB_T`, `THROB_A`).

## Food combos + the stamina bar

Quick-succession eats chain: an eat within `COMBO.WINDOW` s of the last
increments the combo counter, otherwise it resets to 1. Patch grains refresh
the window but do NOT increment (a single swoop would otherwise jump to 20x).

- **Counter popups**: from 2x, each link pops a brief counter — "2x" "3x"
  "4x", then "5x!" "6x!" … (bang from `COMBO.BANG_AT`) — in `#combo`,
  top-center under the level-up popups, styled on the level-up pattern,
  colored by the eaten food's axis. Re-triggering replaces the popup (a
  counter, not a queue).
- **Escalating feel**: the eat flash/shake gain `+COMBO.FX_MUL` per link
  (capped `FX_CAP`), sparkles.burst confetti in the axis color grows with the
  tier, and the eel gets an **excitement surge** (`eel.excite()`) — a brief
  wiggle-amplitude boost that decays over ~a second.
- **Reward (PLACEHOLDER)**: `comboReward(n)` in main.js charges boost stamina
  by `COMBO.STAMINA_PER` per link. The real reward is TBD (Matt) — keep the
  reward inside that one function.
- **Stamina bar**: a slim electric-blue bar riding below the eel
  (`sparkles.StaminaBar`, glow layer — it must read in dark water). Fades in
  while stamina < 1 (draining or recharging) or briefly when a combo charges
  it, fades out when full. Track + fill, ~90×5 px, no HUD chrome.

## The red beans & rice patch

A rare patch-type food (`FOODS.beansrice`, `patch: true`): one spawn is a
drifting cloud of ~26 individual grains (dark-red bean ellipses + off-white
rice capsules — procedural SVG, no assets; swap for art later if wanted). The
patch center falls/sways like any food; grains hold seeded offsets in a
~“blobby ellipse” around it with a slow internal stir. The eel swoops
through it like a whale through krill: grains near the open mouth are eaten
individually (instant shrink-out, tiny grant each, occasional mini-pulse),
the probe sees grains so the jaw opens on approach. All grains eaten → the
patch despawns; otherwise it exits out the bottom like everything else.
Grade rolls once per patch and shades every grain (buzz/throb per grain,
shared phase). Axis LIFE, first guess — retune in the CSV.

## The LOVE axis

The fifth axis. `AXES.love` — **warm coral-red**, K first-guessed for the
usual 4-session pacing (greeting is optional play, so this one is the least
calibrated; retune freely). LOVE is charged by **successful greets**: each
responding critter grants `GREET.LOVE_PER`, capped at `GREET.LOVE_CAP` per
greet (critter greet cooldowns already bound the farm rate). Spontaneous
greets grant nothing (no feedback loop).

- Plumbing: URL override `?love=`, pause meter, title footer, persistence —
  all ride the existing per-axis loops. Old saves default LOVE to 0.
- **First effect** — `DIALS.spontGreet` (LOVE level 2): nearby critters
  (inside `SPONT.RANGE`, off greet-cooldown, on screen) spontaneously greet
  the eel — full in-character response (hearts, jelly heartbeat, seahorse
  pirouette, octo-camouflage, lure flare) but **no befriend-follow** and no
  LOVE grant. Rate `SPONT.RATE`/s per critter at full dial.
- `LEVEL_NOTES.love` authored; the note↔dial alignment test extends to it.

## Seafloor on every plane (+ far-plane fog; the blur is dead)

`TERRAIN` is now keyed per plane — `front / main / near / far` — with average
heights ordered front lowest → far highest (docs/07 P4 item 3), and per-plane
shape (`TERRAIN.POW`: main rolls as dunes at 1.7, the rest mostly hug low):

- **front** (SVG, fgplane.js): a low dark sliver of rolling floor drawn under
  the front kelp, occluding the eel — pooled path per chunk.
- **main** (GL, pf = 1): a rolling dune silhouette behind the eel, drawn with
  the main kelp pass. Kelp/seagrass keep their floor roots and emerge from
  the dunes (visual only — no collision; rocks are the interactive floor).
- **near / far**: unchanged geometry, drawn **sharp**. The depth-of-field
  blur planned here (one FBO, docs/07 decision) was built three ways —
  bilinear upsample, sparse taps, true separable gaussian — and every
  variant read badly in the real scene, so the whole routine was removed
  (see the follow-up section). Depth is faked with **fog**: `LAYERS.FAR.FOG`
  pulls the far plane's palette an extra fraction toward the water color.
  The no-framebuffer rule (docs/03) stands unbroken.

## Rocks + the dressing shaker

- **Seeded rocks** (`worldgen.rocksInChunk`, ~one per 2 chunks): irregular
  polygon boulders sitting on the **main-plane terrain**, pooled SVG paths in
  `<g id="rocks">` (sprite layer, under the veil, behind the eel). Size/shape
  per-rock from the seed.
- **Shatter**: a **boost-charging** eel (boost01 + speed past `ROCKS.SMASH_*`)
  whose head hits a rock shatters it: camera shake + flash, bubble burst +
  a warm pulse (placeholder FX — shards can come later), and the reveal.
- **Reveal — the dressing shaker** (decided 2026-07-05): a little procedural
  SVG shaker pops out with a hop, bobs in place, and is collected on eel
  contact → **greens buff**: for `ROCKS.BUFF_T` seconds the greens food's
  grant is ×`ROCKS.BUFF_MUL`. Feedback: spring-green flash + a guide-style
  popup ("DRESSING! …"). No persistent buff indicator yet (noted for later).
- **Persistence** (decided 2026-07-05): localStorage
  `eel-madness:rocks:v1` = a list of `[x, timestamp]` pairs for shattered
  rocks; a rock respawns after **24 h** (stale entries pruned on load), and
  reset clears the list. Rocks are keyed by their seeded x.

## Two new kelp types (main plane)

Typed from a **side hash-stream** (`hash01(chunk·131+i, salt)`), NOT extra
chunk-RNG draws — the strand stream must stay byte-identical so growth
superset/determinism (check-worldgen) and seahorse anchors hold. The strand
record gains `type` and `hMul`; consumers use `h · hMul`.

- **sinuous** (~18%): taller (`hMul` ~1.35), narrower, with a static
  build-time S-curve along its height — reads long and ribbony under the
  shared shader sway.
- **spindle** (~8%): very tall (`hMul` ~1.6) and very narrow, with periodic
  **growth nodes** up the stalk, each sprouting a fan of little grassy
  projections. The projections inherit the stalk's `frac` (± a small spread)
  and phase, so they ride the stalk's sway exactly and wave differentially —
  no separate sim.
- Lantern bulbs only ever kindle on **normal** strands (typed strands moved
  or reshaped would strand the bulbs).

## Follow-up pass (Matt's playtest, 2026-07-05)

- **Grains +80%**, rice a touch thinner, dispersion +~13% (`patch.rx/ry`);
  Matt raised the grain count to 106 — a real cloud.
- **Blur: REMOVED after three attempts** (Matt, 2026-07-05). Attempt 1
  (⅙-res bilinear upsample) flickered — sub-texel strands undersampled;
  attempt 2 (sparse rotated-grid taps) ghosted; attempt 3 (true separable
  gaussian) still read badly on the real scene. The whole FBO routine is
  deleted — the no-framebuffer rule (docs/03) stands again. Depth is now
  faked with **fog**: `LAYERS.FAR.FOG` pulls the far plane's palette an
  extra fraction toward the water color. Planes draw sharp.
- **Main floor rolls** — `TERRAIN.POW` is per-plane now; main uses 1.4 (vs
  2.6) + AMP 0.34 after two rounds ("still not seeing much variation"), and
  its palette got its own bright dune tones (0.42/0.20 toward surface) —
  the subtle water-mix read as no floor at all under the abyss veil.
  **Rocks ~2×** (R 48–88) and **anchored**: a rock rests on the LOWEST
  terrain top under its footprint (3 samples) with its center only 0.15 r
  above it, so the underside buries into slopes instead of hovering.
- **The main terrain is SOLID now** (Matt, 2026-07-05): `worldgen.mainFloorY`
  is the one authority on the surface height, and (a) the **eel** treats it
  as the floor — the soft wall-steer and hard clamp follow the terrain under
  and just ahead of the head, so it swims up dune faces instead of through
  them; (b) **fish** bottom-clamps ride it too (each species keeps its old
  clearance margin), and the seahorse perch math follows its kelp's new
  root; (c) **kelp and seagrass root ON the terrain surface** (+6 px sink)
  instead of the fixed world floor — lantern bulbs replicate the same root.
  Deliberately NOT solid: falling food still sinks through and despawns
  (darkness-loss gameplay unchanged), and the terrain height still scales
  with the view height, so the surface shifts slightly on resize (accepted —
  same as the rendering).
- **The dressing is real art** — `assets/modifier_dressing.png` (Matt),
  drawn at 2.5× the old procedural placeholder.
- **The abyss hides the new floor** — by design (`VEIL.END_A` keeps the
  bottom at ~7% even at LIGHT 30), so the seafloor/rocks were invisible.
  Matt's call: keep the dark, add the light —
- **EEL LIGHT (new power, EEL MAGIC level 14)**: ambient light radiating
  gently around the eel, flareable by holding **J** (or the touch **✦**
  button). Implementation is deliberately NOT the old cut glow blob: the
  light is a **soft radial mask hole in the veil itself** (`veil.update`'s
  `hole` arg — CSS `mask-image`, rebuilt per frame only while unlocked), so
  the dark is genuinely revealed rather than painted over. Ambient
  radius/strength ride the `eelLight` dial; the flare swells both
  (`EEL_LIGHT` in tuning.js) and adds a faint icy-cyan halo on the glow
  layer (`sparkles.EelHalo`) purely as the "I'm doing this" flourish.
  Watch mobile perf: the per-frame mask string is the one new DOM cost.
- **Main terrain/rocks contrast** bumped so they read once lit.
- **Greet responses**: jelly heartbeat pulses brighter (`J_BEAT_A` 1.1,
  `J_BEAT_R` 0.2) and every response lasts ~33% longer (`FOLLOW.T` 12,
  `J_BEAT_T` 8, `J_FOLLOW_T` 9.3, `S_SPIN_T` 3.5, `O_CAMO_T` 4.7,
  `A_FLARE_T` 2.0). Heart particle lifetime itself is unchanged.

## Follow-up 2 (Matt's release notes, 2026-07-05)

- **The flare got its oomph** — pressing J/✦ now fires an **ignition pulse**:
  the veil hole overshoots outward (`EEL_LIGHT.PULSE_R`, fully clear at the
  peak) and an icy ring races out and fades on the glow layer (`RING_*`,
  drawn by `EelHalo`), then the held flare settles at the old
  brighter-than-ambient steady state. The pulse envelope
  (`eel.flarePulseEnv`) is fast-attack/long-decay, one-shot per ignition.
- **The flare runs on green light stamina** — `eel.lightStam`, a deliberate
  mirror of the boost rules (hold-to-sustain; drains over `STAM_DUR`;
  recharges over `STAM_RECHARGE` while off; needs the `STAM_MIN` reserve to
  re-ignite; holding through empty never self-retriggers). The state machine
  lives on the eel next to boost (`intent.flare`, injected by main like
  `intent.boost`), so it's headless-testable (`check-eellight`). All knobs
  stay in `tuning.EEL_LIGHT`.
- **Stamina bars stack** (decided 2026-07-05): `StaminaBar` takes a fill
  color (`BAR_COLOR` green for light) and a per-frame **row slot**. Pools
  have a fixed order — boost, then light — but visible bars always compact
  from the top slot (the one position that existed before) with **no gaps**;
  when a bar hides, its neighbor slides up (eased), and a hidden bar snaps
  to its slot invisibly so it never slides in on appear.
- **Steering locks to the first touch** (input.js): the two-finger boost
  gesture was retargeting the eel — any extra finger now only signals boost
  and can never move the target or inherit steering when the first finger
  lifts. This is a bug fix, not the final mobile answer — the mobile control
  scheme (virtual joystick + two-hand layout) is a revamp-planning topic
  (docs/11).

## Follow-up 3 (Matt's release notes, 2026-07-05)

- **Octopus feel** (critters.js): tentacles were too stiff — now **halfway to
  the free float**: body-advection 0.78 → 0.4, settle-tau 0.7 → 1.05 s
  (the jelly's free-float is 1.4 — that's the reference endpoint). And they
  **move more, drift-then-spurt**: the continuous gentle pulsing is replaced
  by long becalmed glides (`O_DRIFT_T` 2.2–5 s) punctuated by a single
  flare→contract jet (`O_SPURT_T`, per-spurt kick 90 px/s — rarer so much
  stronger; the old cycle kicked 26 every 3.6 s). The giant runs the same
  cycle ×`GIANT_SLOW`. A startle dodge now also fires the contraction so the
  body visibly reacts. Covered by `check-octo`.
- **The touch control scheme** (decided 2026-07-05): a **virtual joystick +
  three hold-to-act buttons** (greet ♡ / sprint ⚡ / flare ✦, each appearing
  with its unlock). The joystick pad is a fixed zone; the **stick origin
  floats to wherever the touch first lands inside it**, and intent direction
  = the vector from that origin to the drag point (throttle ramps to full at
  `JOY_R` px — docs/02). On touch devices the joystick is the ONE steering
  authority: field taps do nothing (the tap gesture stays free to become the
  revamp's "aim"). Mouse press-and-hold steering is unchanged. The
  two-finger boost gesture is retired; `getBoost()` is Shift-only and the ⚡
  button rides ui.js → main like greet/flare.
- **Settings menu on the title screen**: currently one setting — **joystick
  side** (left/right segmented toggle; buttons take the opposite side via
  `body.stick-right` in style.css). Persisted at
  `eel-madness:settings:v1`, deliberately separate from progress — a
  progress reset never touches settings.
